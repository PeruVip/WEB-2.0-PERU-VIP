Caregivers Play Many Roles

Provide healthy nutrition

Most people eat less as they age. For this reason, it is necessary for older adults to have a balanced diet that provides macronutrients (carbohydrates, proteins and fats), micronutrients (vitamins, minerals and antioxidants) and fiber.

Promote physical activity

Physical exercise in the elderly offers multiple benefits not only at a physical level, such as improved cardiorespiratory and muscular functions or bone health, but also at a mental and socio-affective level, to feel more energy and reduce the risk of depression.

Stimulate cognitive capacity

Very often, the cognitive abilities of the elderly deteriorate progressively, especially memory, perception and attention. To prevent or delay this deterioration, specialists advise cognitive stimulation.

Take precautions to prevent falls

Falls are the leading cause of hospitalization and trauma-related deaths in the elderly. Therefore, it is of utmost importance to make changes in the structure of the home to meet the demands of aging.
